1 Welcome

Grain Marketing is the server-backed evolution of the Grain-Tracker spreadsheet you have been using. It is the same concept — tracking contracts, positions, deliveries, basis, inventory, and P&L for Holmes Farms' grain marketing program — but rebuilt as a multi-user web application running on the farm's own server.

What changed?

  • Multi-device access. Sign in from your laptop, phone, or tablet. Your data is always in sync.
  • Automatic backups. The PostgreSQL database is backed up daily on the Holmes Farms server. No more worrying about losing a browser's localStorage.
  • Team collaboration. Each user signs in with their own Google account. Multiple people can view and manage marketing data.
  • Encrypted at rest. Your grain data is encrypted with AES-256-GCM before it is stored. Only your PIN or password can unlock it.
  • AI assistant. Ask natural-language questions about your positions, contracts, and hedge percentages.

The app lives at risk.holmes-farms.com and is accessible from any modern browser. You can also install it as a PWA (Progressive Web App) on your phone for an app-like experience.

2 Getting Started

Sign In

  1. Navigate to risk.holmes-farms.com in your browser (Chrome, Safari, Edge, or Firefox).
  2. Click Sign in with Google. Use your @holmesfarmsgp.com Google account. If the Google One Tap prompt appears automatically, you can click your name to sign in instantly.
  3. On first sign-in, you will be prompted to set up encryption. This is a one-time process.

First-Time Encryption Setup

Before you can access any data, you need to create two credentials that protect your grain marketing information:

Credential Purpose Requirements
PIN Quick unlock. You will enter this on a numpad every time you open the app or after 30 minutes of inactivity. 4–8 digits
Master Password Recovery fallback. If you forget your PIN, use this password to unlock instead. 6+ characters
Warning If you forget both your PIN and your master password, your encrypted data cannot be recovered. This is the same security model as Grain-Tracker — the encryption keys are derived from your credentials and are never stored in plaintext on the server.
Tip Pick a PIN you can type quickly on the numpad (you will use it often), and a password you can remember but would not easily guess. Write the password down and keep it somewhere safe.

Basis

The Basis sub-tab shows regional basis data to help you make informed marketing decisions. Select a commodity using the color-coded buttons at the top.

Three Sections

  2. Elevator Basis — Shows posted basis for each elevator you have configured in Settings. If no basis data is available for an elevator, it shows "No posted basis."
  3. Seasonal Basis History — Historical basis trends by month showing current basis, 1-year ago, 2-year ago, 3-year average, and percentile ranking. Stronger-than-average basis is highlighted in green; weaker in red.
Tip Set up your elevator list in Settings first so the Elevator Basis section has data to match against.

7 P&L

The P&L (Profit & Loss) tab gives you a comprehensive view of how your marketing program is performing financially. Use the crop year selector at the top to switch between years.

Summary Cards

Three large cards at the top:

  • Realized P&L — Profit or loss on delivered contracts and closed positions.
  • Unrealized P&L — Mark-to-market P&L on open contracts and open positions (based on current market prices vs. your cost basis).
  • Total P&L — Sum of realized + unrealized. Green for profit, red for loss.
Per-Commodity Breakdown

A table showing realized and unrealized P&L split by commodity. Each row shows contract realized, contract unrealized, position realized, position unrealized, and net P&L. A totals row sums everything.

Contract P&L Detail

Every contract with a calculable effective price is listed individually with: commodity, type, buyer, bushels, effective price, cost basis, P&L per bushel, and total P&L.

The cost basis comes from Settings (see section 9). If you have not set a cost basis for a commodity, it defaults to $0.

Position P&L Detail

Each futures/options position is listed with entry price, current or closed price, number of contracts, side (Long/Short), and calculated P&L.

8 Documents

Upload and organize PDF copies of grain contracts and settlement statements. Documents can be linked to specific contracts for quick reference.

Document Types

  • Contract — Confirmation PDFs from elevators/buyers
  • Settlement — Final settlement statements after delivery

How to Upload

  1. Click Upload Document in the toolbar.
  2. Select a PDF file from your device.
  3. Choose the document type (Contract or Settlement).
  4. Optionally add notes.
  5. Click Upload. The server will store and process the file.

Parsed Data

After upload, the server-side AI parser attempts to extract structured data from the PDF — things like commodity, bushels, price, buyer name, and dates. This parsed data appears in a preview column on the documents table. Click "more" to expand all extracted fields.

Linking to Contracts

Documents can be linked to specific contracts. Linked documents show the contract details (type, buyer, bushels) right in the documents table.

Summary Cards

9 Settings

Settings control how the app filters data and calculates your marketing metrics. Changes save immediately when you modify a field or click Save.

Active Crop Year

Select the crop year you are currently managing. All pages (Dashboard, Marketing, P&L, etc.) filter their data to this year. You can also override the crop year on individual pages using their toolbar dropdowns.

Commodities

Shows the 4 active commodities: Corn, Soybeans, Wheat, Heating Oil. Configuration for adding or removing commodities is planned for a future update.

Cost Basis

Enter your cost of production per bushel (or per gallon for Heating Oil) for each commodity. This is used in the P&L calculations to determine profit per bushel:

P&L per bu = Effective Price − Cost Basis

Important If you do not set a cost basis, P&L defaults to comparing against $0. Set this accurately for meaningful P&L numbers.
Bushels Per Contract

The standard contract size for each commodity. Defaults:

CommodityDefault
Corn5,000 bu
Soybeans5,000 bu
Wheat5,000 bu
Heating Oil42,000 gal

Adjust if your elevator uses non-standard sizes.

Gross Bushels

Total expected production per commodity for the active crop year. This is the denominator in the Dashboard's hedge percentage calculation:

Hedge % = Committed Bushels ÷ Gross Bushels × 100

Warning If gross bushels is 0 or not set for a commodity, the hedge % will show as "N/A" or 0%. Always set this at the beginning of the marketing year.
Elevators / Buyers

Enter one elevator or buyer name per line. These names populate the buyer dropdown when creating contracts and deliveries, and they are used in the Basis tab's Elevator Basis section to match against regional basis data.

Examples:

ADM Petersburg Cargill Beardstown CHS Jacksonville Bunge Havana

Click Save Elevators after editing the list.

10 AI Assistant

A chat widget appears in the bottom-right corner of every page. Click it to open a conversation with the AI assistant.

What Can You Ask?

The AI has access to 16 specialized tools that can query your grain marketing data. Example questions:

  • "What's my corn hedge percentage?"
  • "Show me all open contracts"
  • "How many soybeans bushels are unpriced?"
  • "What's my total unrealized P&L for 2026?"
  • "List deliveries this month"
  • "What basis levels are available at ADM?"
  • "Summarize my marketing position"

How It Works

When you send a message, the AI uses Claude (Anthropic's language model) to understand your question, then calls the appropriate tools to query your data from the server. Responses stream in real-time. The AI can chain multiple queries together to answer complex questions.

Conversation History

Your conversations are saved automatically. When you reopen the chat widget, you can see your recent conversation history and continue where you left off.

Note The AI has read-only access to your data. It can look up contracts, positions, and market data, but it cannot create, modify, or delete anything on your behalf.

11 Security

Grain Marketing uses a layered security model to protect your financial data.

Authentication
  • Google Sign-In — Only @holmesfarmsgp.com accounts are accepted.
  • Server sessions — Your login session is stored in PostgreSQL, not in a cookie. It persists until you sign out.
Encryption

Your grain marketing data is encrypted using AES-256-GCM (the same standard used by banks and government agencies). Here is how the key chain works:

  1. Your password is combined with a random 32-byte salt using PBKDF2 (310,000 iterations, SHA-256) to derive a 256-bit master encryption key.
  2. Your PIN is similarly derived into a PIN key, which is used to wrap (encrypt) the password. This way the server stores a PIN-wrapped copy of the password, never the password itself.
  3. Hashes of both PIN and password are stored for verification (100,000 PBKDF2 iterations).
  4. When you enter your PIN, the app derives the PIN key, unwraps the password, then derives the master key. The master key is held in your browser session for the duration of the unlock.
  5. Data is encrypted/decrypted on the server using the session-held key. When you lock the app or sign out, the key is wiped.

The bottom line: nobody can read your data without your PIN or password — not even server administrators.

Auto-Lock

After 30 minutes of inactivity (no clicks, keystrokes, or touch events), the app automatically locks and clears all sensitive data from memory. You will need to re-enter your PIN to continue.

You can also lock manually by clicking the lock button in the header area, or simply close the browser tab.

PIN Lockout

After 5 failed PIN attempts, the app locks you out for 5 minutes. This prevents brute-force guessing. You can always bypass the lockout by switching to the password entry screen ("Forgot PIN?" link).

13 FAQ / Troubleshooting

Q: What if I forget my PIN?
On the lock screen, click "Forgot PIN?" to switch to the password entry screen. Enter your master password to unlock.
Q: What if I forget both my PIN and password?
Unfortunately, your encrypted data cannot be recovered. This is the same as Grain-Tracker — the encryption keys are derived from your credentials and are never stored in plaintext. You would need to create a new encryption setup, which starts with a fresh data set.
Q: Can I use this on my phone?
Yes. The app is fully responsive and works on any screen size. For the best mobile experience, you can install it as a PWA (Progressive Web App):

Android (Chrome): Tap the three-dot menu and select "Add to Home screen."
iPhone (Safari): Tap the Share button and select "Add to Home Screen."

This gives you an app icon on your home screen and a full-screen experience without the browser chrome.
Q: Where is my data stored?
Your data is encrypted and stored in a PostgreSQL database on the Holmes Farms server (a Dell PowerEdge R660xs in the office). The database is backed up daily. Your data never leaves the farm's own infrastructure (except when transmitted over HTTPS to your browser).
Q: Can multiple people use it?
Yes. Each person signs in with their own @holmesfarmsgp.com Google account. Each user has their own encryption credentials and data access.
Q: What happened to the original Grain-Tracker?
It is still available and works offline as always. Your data can be imported from Grain-Tracker into the new system using the import script (contact Adam). After import, you can continue using either app, but the server-backed version is the recommended go-forward platform.
Q: The app shows "No market data available" on the Dashboard.
This means the market data feed has not returned prices yet. Click the Refresh button in the Position Summary header, or wait for the next auto-refresh cycle (every 5 minutes). If it persists, the market data API may be temporarily unavailable.
Q: How do I change my PIN or password?
Currently, PIN and password are set during initial encryption setup and cannot be changed through the UI. Contact Adam if you need to reset your encryption credentials.
Q: The lock screen appeared while I was working. Did I lose anything?
No. The auto-lock triggers after 30 minutes of inactivity. Any changes you had already saved (clicked a Save button or the data was auto-saved) are safe on the server. Simply re-enter your PIN to continue where you left off. Unsaved form data in an open modal may be lost.
Q: Can I switch between light and dark mode?
Yes. Click the sun/moon icon button in the top-right corner of the header. Your preference is saved in your browser and persists across sessions.
